My Design Philosophy ↓

Why design?

In short, I love questions. My first loves were language and journalism and I found myself so deeply attracted to them because I wanted to know what questions to ask and how to ask them.

Design is all about knowing the right questions to ask and even more fun, asking the wrong ones. Answers are fun and they matter, but the process of finding your question is the key.


What is design?

Design is a universal language that affects every daily interaction.

Design is a chain between the most complicated mathematical theorems and the most abstract philosophical theories.

Design is alive. It's uniquely capable of growth and adaptation and the best designs are those that are never finished.


How do I design?

I design for people. I design with the user in mind - both I am akin to and those whom I couldn't be more different from.

I design iteratively. Designs are the product of hours of considerations, rediesigns, quick follow-up emails, and total scraps.

I design imperfectly. I'm never 100% satisfied with my designs because as the world grows, so does design.
